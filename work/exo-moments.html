campaign landing page: balancing creativity with usability

ui/ux designer & integrator

|

2 weeks

this project involved creating the first-ever landing page for the exo autumn campaign. the core challenge was to design a highly flexible, blog-style page capable of seamlessly integrating diverse media (videos, articles, images) while maintaining a structured, user-friendly, and mobile-responsive design. the goal was to support the campaign's theme: encouraging public transit use by showing passengers they can "ride along" with exo and take time for themselves.

the problem

the requirement was to design a brand-new landing page for the autumn campaign that satisfied the marketing team's need for a multi-media, blog-like experience. this meant creating a structure that could easily incorporate various content types (videos, articles, links, audio) and adapt to new content releases over the campaign's duration, all while delivering an intuitive and mobile-friendly scrolling experience.

goals

the main goals for this project were to:

  • support campaign strategy: create a high-impact page to encourage public transit ridership by highlighting the benefit of saving time.

  • modular content integration: design a flexible system that allows the mkarketing team to easily add new content (acting as a "blog" container).

  • mobile-first usability: ensure the page offers an anjoyable, smooth, and easily scannable scrolling experience on mobile devices.

my process

my process focused on establishing a solid foundation for design coherence and content flexibility.

foundational research

as this was my first project, i conducted extensive research into the existing exo website's look-and-feel and component library to ensure the new landing page was completely coherent with the brand aesthetic.

component strategy

i created a detailed document containing a table of reusable components (text blocks, image containers, media embeds) and their intended use. this became the basis for the design.

prototyping & flexbility

i provided the marketing team with a figma component library and two different layout prototypes to demonstrate the page's modular flexibility, gaining quick approval.

initial information architecture

mid-campaign integration challenge

as the campaign progressed, three youtube videos (one portrait and two landscape) were released at different times. i successfully integrated them right after the campaign visuals for maximum discoverability, rather than burying them at the page's end.

responsive media solution

the mixed aspect ratios of the videos presented a major mobile challenge. i designed and integrated a custom responsive layout to cleanly display the three videos across all device sizes.

figma - prototype of the mobile version fo the landing page

the solution

the final landing page successfully bridged the marketing's creative vision with core usability principles:

  • blog-style flexibility: the component-based structure allows the marketing team to easily update the page with new articles and media, achieving the primary goal of the project.

  • prioritized content: strategic placement of the campaign visuals and newly released videos high on the page ensures they capture immediate user attention, optimizing the flow of information.

  • responsive multi-media grid: i solved the complex integration of mixed aspect ratio videos by developing a robust, mobile-friendly grid. this ensures a clean, enjoyable scrolling experience on any mobile device, a key objective for the campaign.

  • high-contrast footer: included recommended articles and a prominent newsletter sign-up form in the footer to drive secondary conversions.

reflections & learnings

the biggest challenge was overcoming the initial steep learning curve of building a professional web page design from scratch. the invaluable guidance from coworkers, combined with proactive research (building the component inventory), allowed me to succeed. a key technical learning was that foundational importance of mobile responsiveness and how information architecture (prioritizing critical content at the top) is essential for maintaining user attention on long, blog-style pages.
